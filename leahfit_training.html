Training Principles

Core guidelines for functional training at 60+

🌬️
Breath First
Every movement effort begins with exhalation. If breathing breaks down, reduce intensity — never hold your breath to compensate.
📈
Progressive Overload
Increase weight or reps by 5–10% each week. Safety over challenge — only progress when you've fully adapted.
⏸️
Full Recovery
For 60+, rest 90–120 seconds between sets. Rest at least 1 day after every 2 training days. Listen to your body.
🎯
Quality Over Quantity
10 perfect reps beat 20 compensated ones. Feel the target muscle activating — don't just count reps.
💧
Hydration & Nutrition
2L water daily, one glass before and after training. Protein target: bodyweight (kg) × 1.2g/day to support muscle synthesis.
🛑
Pain = Stop
Distinguish "muscle soreness" (normal) from "joint pain / sharp pain" (stop immediately). Reduce load at any sign of discomfort.

Rib Flare · Special Notes

Understanding your condition makes your training more targeted

🔍 Root Cause of Rib Flare

Usually caused by chronic chest breathing, TVA inhibition, and extension-pattern pelvic compensation. The diaphragm lifts excessively upward, the ribs lose inward control, resulting in a "flared" position.

✅ Core Correction Logic

Restore diaphragmatic descent, activate the lower obliques, allowing the ribs to naturally draw inward and downward on exhalation. This is a neural repatterning process — it takes 4–8 weeks of consistent practice.

🫁 Connection to Breathlessness

When ribs are flared, the thorax is "locked" in an inhale position, the diaphragm can't descend fully, resulting in under-utilisation of lung capacity and breathlessness. Correct breathing training directly addresses this.

⚠️ Training Cautions

Avoid excessive spinal extension movements (e.g. over-arching the back). All core training paired with exhale on effort. While standing, consciously check if ribs are "flying out".
